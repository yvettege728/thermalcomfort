S02 · Recommended path · Phase mockup
Four phases of the feedback loop
1
Phase 1 · M0 – M9
Metric Collection
Temperature sensors and occupant survey data are made visible for the first time. The building's thermal profile becomes legible — not to users yet, but to the research layer. Evidence is the first condition of action.
0%
2
Phase 2 · M3 – M18
Deploy Feedback
QR voting and the public dashboard go live. Occupants can signal discomfort in under 10 seconds. Isolation drops as social proof becomes visible: "73 others on this tray voted cold this week." Friction-to-voice falls from 5 min to ~10 sec.
0%
3
Phase 3 · M6 – M24
Evaluate Feedback
Aggregated signals cross thresholds that trigger formal review. Facilities sees clustered patterns, not individual complaints. The loop is closing: perception gap narrows because the right people now see the right data at the right time.
0%
4
Phase 4 · M12 – M48
Implement Change
Named interventions — setpoint adjustments, riser reroutes — are recorded publicly against the feedback that motivated them. Accountability is no longer implicit. Steam co-benefits appear late, as a side-effect of governance, not the goal.

What the system now shows you
●
70% of occupants thought they were the only one feeling cold. They were wrong — the signal was always collective, just invisible. M02 Isolation Index · P2 survey · n = 42
●
The current process to flag thermal discomfort takes over 5 minutes and 7 steps — most people never complete it. S02 reduces this to a single tap. M03 Friction-to-Voice · Harvard Facilities service request audit
●
Without the feedback system, the average time from a complaint to visible action is 18 days. Visibility alone reduced this to 4–8 days in comparable building deployments. M04 Loop Closure · Goetz 2011; Darby 2006 feedback review
$
Each hour of thermal discomfort equals $51.14 of paid-but-unusable tuition time. Across the school, the current gap represents ~$5.1M/year of institutional value leaking invisibly. $90k/yr ÷ 10mo ÷ 22d ÷ 8h = $51.14/h · Harvard GSD tuition 2025–26
